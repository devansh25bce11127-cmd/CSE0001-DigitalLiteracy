# Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist for College Students

With the rapid growth of digital services, students frequently use the internet for banking, shopping, social networking, and academic work. While these services offer convenience, they also expose users to cyber threats such as phishing, identity theft, and online payment fraud. The following checklist provides practical steps that college students in India can follow to stay safe online.

## General Online Safety Tips

1. **Use Strong Passwords**

Create strong passwords that include uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Avoid using simple passwords such as birthdays or names.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**

Whenever possible, enable two-factor authentication for your email, banking, and social media accounts. This adds an extra layer of security.

3. **Avoid Suspicious Links**

Do not click on suspicious links received through emails, SMS messages, or social media. These links may lead to phishing websites designed to steal your personal information.

4. **Install Apps from Trusted Sources**

Download applications only from official platforms such as the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid installing apps from unknown websites.

5. **Keep Devices Updated**

Regularly update your phone, laptop, and applications. Updates often include security patches that protect against newly discovered vulnerabilities.

6. **Be Careful on Public Wi-Fi**

Avoid accessing banking apps or making financial transactions while connected to public Wi-Fi networks. Hackers may intercept sensitive information.

## UPI and Financial Safety Tips

7. **Never Share Your UPI PIN or OTP**

Your UPI PIN and One-Time Password (OTP) are confidential. Banks and payment companies will never ask for these details.

8. **Remember: QR Codes Are Used to Send Money**

Scanning a QR code always initiates a payment. If someone asks you to scan a QR code to receive money, it is likely a scam.

9. **Verify Payment Requests**

Before approving any payment request in your UPI app, carefully check the name and details of the recipient.

10. **Avoid Unknown Payment Requests**

Do not approve payment requests sent by unknown individuals or businesses.

## Reporting Cybercrime

If you become a victim of cybercrime or suspect online fraud, report it immediately using the official government channels below.

**National Cyber Crime Portal:**  
https://cybercrime.gov.in

**Cybercrime Financial Fraud Helpline:**  
1930

Reporting incidents quickly increases the chances of stopping fraudulent transactions and recovering lost money.
